    Indicator 5.1.1. Sex ratio at birth

    June 2021

    General Statistics Office

    Sex ratio at birth is the number of boys per 100 girls out of total live births during the reporting period (usually one year) of an area.

    number of boys per 100 girls

    The list of administrative units in Vietnam issued together with the Prime Minister's Decision No. 124/2004/QD-TTg dated July 8, 2004 and the changes updated by the General Statistics Office to date December 31, 2019)

    - Population and housing census;

    - Census midterm;

    - Population change and family planning survey;

    - Administrative data.

    The indicator “Sex ratio at birth” is compiled from data from the Population and Housing Census (10-year cycle), the mid-term population and housing census (5-year cycle), and extrapolated from the results of the annual population change - family planning sample survey. The above censuses and censuses are all part of the National Statistical Census Program and conducted by the General Statistics Office. In addition, this indicator is also compiled from administrative data of the Ministry of Health.

    Year

    General Statistics Office;

    General Statistics Office;

    The sex ratio at birth (also known as the sex ratio at birth) reflects the sex balance of the number of new born children in a period (usually a calendar year). Normally, this ratio ranges from 103 to 107 and is stable over time and space. Any change in this ratio that deviates from the normal range reflects an intentional intervention to some extent and affects the natural sex imbalance, threatening population instability. number.

    Method of computation

    Annual data from 2010-2019 by urban/rural and regional breakdown.

    (Published source: Statistical Yearbook of the General Statistics Office)

    The indicator “Sex ratio at birth” reflects the sustainable development goals of Vietnam (target 5.1). The set of global sustainable development indicators does not have this indicator.
